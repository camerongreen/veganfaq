Don't plants have feelings?

Answers below.

Answers

  • Most creatures we eat are herbivorous so we save more plants by consuming them directly.
  • Studies show plants can respond to stimuli, however lacking a central nervous system and brain there is no evidence they feel pain.
  • The line between plants and animals is blurry. Mussels, sea anemones, mushrooms, all sit in the middle. Ultimately our best scientific knowledge should be our guide here, erring on the side of compassion.

What would happen to all the cows?

Answers below

Answers

  • The breeding of farm animals is orchestrated by humans for maximum profitability, even where artificial insemination is not used.
  • Within one generation, without this breeding, numbers would reduce dramatically.
  • If people wished, they would be able to maintain breeds of farm animals as companion animals.
  • Freed up land available for more crops, sanctuaries and reclamation by nature (perhaps with our help).

Further reading

  • "Cattle Breeding Guide" LiveCorp/Meat Livestock Australia. Oct 2010. https://www.livecorp.com.au/LC/files/e4/e4a91e28-4b11-45c2-bff4-db9d83faa555.pdf

Why should we help animals when humans are suffering?

Answers below

Answers

  • Being vegan and advocating for veganism helps achieve tangible change towards a more sustainable and socially equitable world
  • No one would ask why we were helping people with severe intellectual disabilities instead of those with high IQs.
  • Most vegans have a wide circle of compassion.
  • Many people do nothing for humans or animals, so why criticise people doing something positive.

Limits of rational argumentation

Answers below

Answers

  • Unlikely we will change someone's mind in a single discussion.
  • The idea that if we have truth on our side and provide an irrefutable argument for that truth, people will change, is optimistic at best.
  • People will feel their ethics are being attacked, which they are, and will become defensive.
  • Thus it is important for us to remember to bring our other social skills to the table when discussing ethical considerations.
